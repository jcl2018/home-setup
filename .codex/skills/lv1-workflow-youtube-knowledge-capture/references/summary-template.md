# YouTube Knowledge Summary Template

Use this shape unless the user asks for something different:

## What This Video Teaches

- State the core thesis in 2-4 sentences.
- Name the audience or context when it is obvious from the video.

## Key Ideas

- Extract the main concepts, frameworks, or arguments.
- Keep each point self-contained and concrete.
- Separate the speaker's claims from your own inference when needed.

## Evidence And Examples

- Call out the strongest supporting examples, anecdotes, demos, or case studies.
- Mention notable timestamps when the transcript provides a clear boundary.

## Practical Takeaways

- Turn the material into actions, heuristics, or checklists when the video supports that.
- If the video is mostly conceptual, say that clearly instead of forcing fake action items.

## Caveats And Open Questions

- Mention missing context, weak evidence, obvious bias, or transcript quality problems.
- Flag when the video has no captions and the summary had to rely on a weaker source.

## Optional Timestamped Outline

- Add a short timestamped outline when the user asks for study notes, a chapter view, or a recap that should be easy to revisit.
- Keep it coarse-grained; do not mirror every caption segment.
